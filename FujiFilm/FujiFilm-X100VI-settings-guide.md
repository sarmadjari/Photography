**C1 • Street**

## Introduction

The FujiFilm X100VI features seven Custom Setting slots (C1–C7) accessible via the mode dial. Each slot saves virtually all camera settings, enabling instant switching between fully optimized configurations. The X100VI's 40MP X-Trans CMOS 5 HR sensor, 23mm f/2 lens (35mm equiv.), built-in 4-stop ND filter, and IBIS make it uniquely versatile as a compact everyday camera.

This guide covers four essential profiles: Street Photography, Portraits, Travel & Landscape, and Night & Low Light.

---

## Performance & Power Management (Set These First)

These settings apply globally and are recommended by virtually every pro X100VI shooter. Configure them before saving any Custom Settings.

### Boost Mode

| Menu Path | Setting | Value |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Setup → Power Management | Performance | **Boost** |
| Setup → Power Management | Boost Mode Setting | **Frame Rate Priority** (street/action) or **Resolution Priority** (landscape/portraits) |

> Boost Mode increases EVF refresh rate up to 100fps and speeds up AF acquisition. It drains the battery faster — always carry a spare. For low-light shooting, switch to **Low Light Priority** for a brighter, less noisy live view.

### Quick Start & Power Saving

| Menu Path | Setting | Value |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Setup → Power Management | Quick Start Mode | **On** |
| Setup → Power Management | Auto Power Off | **5 min** (or longer for street) |
| Setup → Power Management | High Performance Mode | **On** |

> Extending Auto Power Off prevents the camera from sleeping and missing quick street moments. Quick Start Mode dramatically reduces wake time from sleep.

### Recommended Custom Button (Fn) Assignments

| Button | Assignment | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| **Fn1** (top plate) | Film Simulation | Instant look switching without diving into menus |
| **Fn2** (rear) | Focus Check / Magnifier | Quick MF verification — essential for zone focusing |
| **Rear Command Dial Press** | White Balance | Fast WB shifts for changing light |
| **Touch Fn** (swipe) | Focus Area Move | Drag the AF point across the screen |
| **Q Menu** | Add Boost Mode, ND Filter, Grain Effect, Self-Timer | Customize Q Menu with your most-changed settings |

> Pro photographers stress that custom button setup is what separates fast shooters from menu divers. Assign your most-changed settings to Fn buttons and add everything else to the Q Menu.

## Useful Tricks & Hidden Settings

### Half-Press Shutter to Focus (Default Behavior)

By default, the X100VI focuses when you half-press the shutter button in AF-S or AF-C mode. If this isn't working, check:

| Menu Path | Setting | Value |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Setup → Button/Dial Setting | Shutter AF | **On** |
| (Focus mode lever on the camera body) | Focus Mode | **AF-S** or **AF-C** (not M) |

> Half-press = focus lock. Full press = take the shot. This is the standard behavior in AF-S mode. If you want to separate focus from the shutter entirely, see Back-Button Focus below.

### Back-Button Focus (Separate Focus from Shutter)

Many pros prefer decoupling focus from the shutter button so a half-press only meters, not focuses. This gives you full control over when focus locks.

| Step | Menu Path | Setting |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Setup → Button/Dial Setting → Shutter AF | Set **AF-S** to **Off** and **AF-C** to **Off** |
| 2 | Setup → Button/Dial Setting → Function (Fn) Setting → **AE-L / AF-L button** | Assign **AF-ON** |

> Now the shutter button only meters and fires. Press the rear **AE-L/AF-L** button to focus. This is especially powerful for street and portrait work — focus once, recompose freely, and fire without re-focusing.

### Show Grid Overlay on Screen & Viewfinder

Enabling the rule-of-thirds grid (or other overlays) requires two steps:

| Step | Menu Path | Setting |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Setup → Screen Setting → **Disp. Custom Setting** | Check **Framing Guideline** (enable the checkbox) |
| 2 | Setup → Screen Setting → **Framing Guideline** | Choose: **Grid 9** (rule of thirds), **Grid 24** (fine grid), or **HD Framing** (16:9 overlay) |

> **Grid 9** (rule of thirds) is the most popular choice. The grid appears on both the rear LCD and the EVF. You must enable it in Disp. Custom Setting first — it won't show up otherwise.

### Electronic Level (Horizon Guide)

| Menu Path | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| Setup → Screen Setting → Disp. Custom Setting | Check **Electronic Level** |

> A horizontal/vertical level indicator appears in the viewfinder and screen. Essential for landscapes, architecture, and seascapes. You can also assign it to an Fn button for quick toggling.

### Quick Focus Mode Switching (Physical Lever)

The focus mode lever on the front of the camera body has three positions:

| Lever Position | Behavior |
|---------------|----------|
| **S** | AF-S — Single autofocus, locks on half-press |
| **C** | AF-C — Continuous autofocus, tracks moving subjects |
| **M** | Manual Focus — use the focus ring; back button still activates AF if configured |

> Flipping to **M** instantly gives you zone focusing for street work without touching any menus. Flip back to **S** for portraits. This physical lever is faster than any menu.

---

### How to Save a Custom Setting

1. Configure all settings in P, A, S, or M mode as desired
2. Press **MENU/OK** → **Camera Settings** → **Edit/Save Custom Setting**
3. Select a slot (**C1–C7**) → Press **MENU/OK** → **Save**
4. Optionally name the preset for easy recall

## C1 — Street Photography

*Optimized for: Fast, discreet shooting with pre-set focus and minimal shutter noise*

### Philosophy

Street photography rewards speed and subtlety. Zone focusing eliminates AF lag entirely — set focus distance and aperture so your depth of field covers the scene. The built-in ND filter enables wide apertures in bright light. Electronic shutter keeps things silent. Classic Chrome or Acros adds the timeless look street work demands.

### Pro Tips for Street

- **Zone Focusing:** At f/8, MF distance ~2m, everything from ~1m to ∞ stays sharp with the 23mm lens
- **Silent Mode:** Assign **Silent Mode** to the Fn button for instant electronic shutter + no AF assist light
- **Pre-set Distance:** Use the distance indicator ring on the lens barrel for fast MF zone adjustments
- **Exposure Comp Dial:** Dial in −1/3 EV on backlit scenes to protect highlights
- **Extend Auto Power Off:** Set to 5 min+ so the camera doesn't sleep between shots — you'll miss moments waiting for it to wake
- **Boost Mode:** Use **Frame Rate Priority** for smoother EVF — critical for tracking moving subjects
- **Spare Batteries:** Boost Mode + Electronic Shutter eats battery; always carry 1–2 spares

- **Boost Mode:** Use **Resolution Priority** for the sharpest EVF preview when checking critical focus on eyes

## C3 — Travel & Landscape

*Optimized for: Maximum resolution, dynamic range, and accurate color rendering*

### Philosophy

Travel and landscape work prioritizes image quality over speed. IBIS lets you shoot handheld at lower shutter speeds while keeping base ISO for maximum dynamic range. Auto ISO is set conservatively (max 1600) so the camera stays at base ISO in good light but has headroom for shade or dusk. The sweet spot for the 23mm lens is f/5.6–f/8 — beyond f/11, diffraction softens the image. Velvia/Vivid saturates colors beautifully for landscapes, while Classic Neg is a strong alternative for travel documentary work.

### Pro Tips for Travel & Landscape

- **Optimal Sharpness:** f/5.6–f/8 is the resolving sweet spot; avoid f/11+ due to diffraction at 40MP
- **IBIS Technique:** Brace the camera with both hands, tuck elbows in, and exhale while pressing the shutter — IBIS + good technique gets sharp shots at 1/15 sec or slower
- **Auto ISO Strategy:** Max 1600 keeps noise low while giving headroom for shade, interiors, and dusk. In bright daylight the camera will sit at ISO 125 anyway
- **Golden Hour WB:** Set White Balance to **Daylight** to preserve warm tones rather than neutralizing them with Auto WB
- **ND Filter:** The built-in 4-stop ND lets you create motion blur (waterfalls, crowds) even handheld in bright daylight at slower shutter speeds
- **Electronic Level:** Assign to an Fn button — essential for architecture and seascapes

## C4 — Night & Low Light (Handheld)

*Optimized for: Handheld night cityscapes, street at night, events, and low-light scenes — leveraging IBIS and the f/2 lens*

### Philosophy

The X100VI is surprisingly capable at night without a tripod. The f/2 lens gathers 4x more light than f/4, and the 5-axis IBIS lets you shoot handheld down to ~1/4 sec with careful technique. Aperture Priority at f/2 with aggressive Auto ISO keeps things simple — the camera handles the math while you focus on composition. Eterna Cinema's muted, low-contrast rendering suits moody night scenes beautifully. Noise Reduction at −4 preserves detail for post-processing; modern denoise tools (Lightroom AI, DxO PureRAW) handle ISO 12800 remarkably well.

### Critical Night-Specific Settings

| Menu Path | Setting | Value | Why |
|-----------|---------|-------|-----|
| Shooting → Image Quality | Long Exposure NR | **Off** | Not needed for handheld (shutter speeds too short to produce hot pixels) |
| Shooting → Image Stabilization | IBIS | **On** | Essential for handheld — enables sharp shots at 1/15–1/4 sec |
| Setup → Power Management | Boost Mode Setting | **Low Light Priority** | Brightens EVF/LCD live view in dark scenes for easier composition and focusing |
| Setup → Screen Setting | Monitor Brightness | **Manual → −2 to −4** | Prevents a bright screen from destroying your night vision and gives a more accurate exposure preview |

### Handheld Night Technique

- **Brace yourself:** Tuck elbows in, exhale slowly, press the camera against your face through the EVF — this plus IBIS can get you sharp shots at 1/8 sec
- **Burst through it:** Switch to **CH (Continuous High)** drive mode momentarily and fire 3–5 frames — pick the sharpest one
- **Lean on surfaces:** Rest the camera on a wall, railing, or table for improvised stability without a tripod

### Pro Tips for Night

- **Mechanical Shutter Always:** Electronic shutter produces banding from flickering artificial lights (LEDs, neon, fluorescent) — always use mechanical at night
- **RAW is Non-Negotiable:** Night shots need shadow recovery and white balance adjustment that JPEGs can't handle
- **AI Denoise in Post:** At ISO 6400–12800, run RAW files through Lightroom AI Denoise or DxO PureRAW — the results at high ISO are dramatically better than in-camera NR
- **Reduce Screen Brightness:** Set to Manual −2 or lower. A bright screen at night ruins your adapted vision and makes images look brighter than they are (leading to underexposure)
- **Battery Warning:** Low Light Priority Boost Mode + bright scenes with lots of AF activity drain batteries faster. Carry 1–2 spares
- **Exposure Comp +1/3 to +2/3:** Night metering tends to underexpose. Dial in positive compensation for neon/city scenes to keep them punchy

### If You Do Use a Tripod (Optional)

For the occasional long exposure (light trails, smooth water at night), override these C4 settings:

| Override | Change To |
|----------|-----------|
| Exposure Mode | **M (Manual)** |
| Shutter Speed | **2–30 sec** |
| ISO | **125–800** (fixed) |
| IBIS | **Off** (prevents micro-vibrations on tripod) |
| Self-Timer | **2 sec** (prevents shake from pressing shutter) |
| Long Exposure NR | **On** (removes hot pixels in exposures over 1 sec) |
